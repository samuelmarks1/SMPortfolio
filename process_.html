Samuel Marks Design

Like most UX design, my design process is iterative and incorporates findings from user research and usability testing to deliver value in every version or release. What makes my process unique is that it's Human Centered and takes into account the complexity of social organizations, the way they are evaluated, the livability, usability, and sustainability of our relationship with technology, and who benefits from design.


I use a wide range of tools and methods to conduct research, design, and collaborate with clients, stakeholders, and owners. I share a sense of pride and ownership with project team members and I use retrospectives to share feedback and improve.


1. Discover

Whether it's product research, market research, or getting to know a project team, Discovery is critical to the UX design process. I like to begin projects by creating user personas, flows, and journey maps. Many discovery activities recur throughout the process, but applying them at the beginning of any project will help generate ideas for research and design further down the road.


Exemplary Design Work: Cater

2. Ideate

During the Ideation stage it's time to dream big or go home! This is when I approach the problem from every angle and generate as many solutions as I can. By measuring and comparing many possible solutions ideas with findings from discovery I can narrow in on the best ones.


Exemplary Ideation Work: Smart City Greenhouse

3. Design

Next is Design. Outlining information architecture and sitemaps are the first steps in translating research and a proposed solution into a functional and useable application. Then, visual and interaction design occur simultaneously as a styleguide is created. With these guidlines in place, I begin creating hi-fi prototypes and detailed specs to communicate design features and decision to clients, stakeholders, owners and leadership.


Exemplary Design Work: Visualizing Business Analytics

4. Evaluate

From log in screens to complex data tables and more, usability tests can be used to explore, assess, compare, and validate different designs. I conduct usability tests by asking users to execute tasks in a controlled environment while being observed and recorded. Then I analyze and interpret the data using benchmarks and other criteria before presenting the findings and making recommendations.


Exemplary Evaluation Work: Kindle App Usability Study

PROCESS

5. Iterate

With every sprint, cycle, and release, I Iterate and repeat the previous steps using knowledge gained to refine and improve the user experience. It's hard to get a design right the first time. That's why every step of the UX process is aimed at incorporating user feedback and refining the design until it is. Reflecting on project work also improves my ability to research, design, scope, and plan.
