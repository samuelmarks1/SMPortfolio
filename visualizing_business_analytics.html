In this study, I participated with 3 others; Juan Cai, Joe Rothlin, and Kendall Sahl, in the research and design of an interactive data visualization tool for a local import and distribution company. It was specifically designed to allow our users to compare the sales performance of different salespeople and sales groups within specified geographical regions, to monitor sales account activity over time, and to help our users communicate important sales information to other stakeholders such as fellow employees, and individual sales groups and salespeople.


My personal responsibilities on this project included acting as liaison to stakeholders and users, data cleaning, and assisting with design.

Discovery & Ideation

To begin, we arranged several meeting with our clients to learn about their sales goals and strategies and the structure of their organization. We were unable to gain access to a large dataset for the first few weeks, which prolonged the discovery phase and forced us to rely on sketches and "dummy data" for explorative usability studies.


     The delay in attaining data made early design difficult, but it also allowed for us to better understand the data before representing with visualization software. Using paper & pencil sketches we were able to verify the depth and accuracy of our requirements, and once we started exploring the capabilities of Tableau with out clients we were quickly able to leverage that understanding.


Project Goals

• Facilitate task driven sales strategy

• Allow comparisons of rep group sales performances by region and state without searching spreadsheets

• Monitor and track sales by last date of activity at a glance

• Communicate important sales information to rep groups and individual salespeople

Design

When we finally acquired the data, it came in three separate excel spreadsheets, one for each year. The major step  in data cleaning was to reformat the data in a way that Tableau would easily understand.


Data

• 197100 rows

• 3 years x 5475 Customers

• 42 Rep groups/Independent Reps

• 177 Individual Salespeople


Dimensions

• Customer number, Name, Address (street, city, state, zip), phone number

• Date (Month)

• Dollars Sold

• Cost of goods sold

• Profit

• Class Trade

• Rep number


Calculated Fields

• Dates of Activity

• Dates of most recent Activity

• 6 month mark (Today – 6 months)

• State grouping

• Rep number grouping


Data Cleaning and Reformatting

• 370+ unrecognized zip codes (now 330+ mapped manually)

• 1 null value customer name

• Reformatted from 3 .xls sheets to one sheet to reduce query execution time and keep interactions within acceptable response times




Evaluation

After cleaning the data, calculating fields, and mapping groups, we tested several different interactive dashboards designed to meet our users' requirements. Each participant was asked to find a specific salesperson and compare that salesperson's performance from this year to date to last year's sales performance to date. Both participants were quickly and easily able to accomplish the set task. Afterward, both participants spent additional time exploring their data for insights, which in turn revealed more usability issues and further insight for design.

Usability Test Findings

• Tree maps are too abstract for our users' hands-on strategy

• Account for geographic occlusion

• Black background VS. white background

• Bar charts for overview of performance by groups

• Line charts for individual performance

• Give context with account activity

Final Iteration

Incorporating the findings from formal usability testing, we presented our final data visualization design to our clients. Featured on a black background to enhance visibility, the visualization shows each salesperson grouped by representative groups and further divided over geographic regions. Performance is compared over time on every scale and individual customer accounts are mapped to flag slow or inactive accounts.

Using the interactive visualization tool we created for them, our users/stakeholders were able to take immediate action on insights gained and affect the bottom line for their organization. In addition they also viewed the tool as a means of communicating vital information to other salespeople and groups. As researchers and designers, we came away more prepared for future data visualization work and ambiguous situations.
