Cater is a contextually-aware recommendation app design that helps users balance their dietary restrictions with their unique dining preferences, offering users better recommendation listings and the ability to find meals they can enjoy in a variety of situations. The design of Cater was also my first opportunity to lead the entire UX process up to development.


Discovery

I identified the need for better dining recommendations after enjoying meals with several friends and family members with specific dietary needs. Observing the steps that they took to find appealing dishes and avoid or control certain ingredients made it clear that current dining recommendation apps were failing to meet the needs of many. 


Contextual Inquiry

In order to gain a deeper understanding of how people were using current systems I conducted a contextual-inquiry study with ten research participants. Participants were recruited via email and recruitment efforts continued after the initial study. While none of the initial participants stated any known dietary restrictions, their stated preferences and behavior highlighted mismatched expectations and opportunities to improve the overall user experience.

Evaluation

Using the designed prototype, I scheduled 2 usability tests with participants that reported having very specific dietary restrictions.

    

     While the prototype scenario was very limited, the usability tests helped me validate needed features for the app:

Gained Insights

Contextual Concerns: Proximity, time of day, social context, familiarity (Comfort).

Interaction: First hand recommendations and access to images restaurant, food, and menus.

Preference: Past experience, good food, great service, ambiance, cuisine, reliability of source.

Competitive Landscape Analysis

With information gathered during the contextual inquiry I designed and analyzed a competitive landscape of twelve related products and interfaces. This included a focus on features of speech interfaces, which eventually became a side note to the larger goal of creating an app that is contextually aware. Products were compared across a range of categories including native software and hardware integration (such as geo-fencing or personal contacts), machine learning, and cost.  

Interaction Model

Next, I created an interaction model to map the user journey based on these findings and stated existing design  opportunities in the respective categories. This model follows the user through the decision making process and illustrates several paths users take to achieve their goals.

Design and Prototyping

Using the findings from the contextual inquiry, competitive analysis, and interaction model, I drafted an information architecture and started designing screens in Sketch. Afterward, I uploaded screens from Sketch to InVision and began prototyping the mobile experience. Retrospective

Leading and executing the user experience design process on my own was a tremendous challenge. This project work was completed over 8 weeks while I was participating in several other projects. Throughout the process, I learned how stages of the UX design process fit together, how to leverage opportunities and gain access to users, and how to interpret, evaluate, and design for users wants and needs.


     Looking forward, research should focus on users in the restaurant and food service industry and system standards for interfacing with diners' specifications. I would also like to incorporate the findings of the research and usability tests using a more robust and data-driven prototyping tool like Axure.


     The Cater project demonstrates a great opportunity to help people affected by food allergies and sensitivities by connecting them with the restaurants and businesses that want to serve them. I would like to thank all of the people that agreed to participate in user research and usability activities for this project!!!






• Participant 1 had Type 1 Diabetes and described methods for monitoring or tracking particular ingredients such as sugars, carbohydrates, and sodium.

• Participant 2 suffered from Rheumatoid Arthritis and managed their pain by avoiding inflammatory foods from the Solanaceae family (i.e. nightshades; potatoes, tomatoes, peppers, etc.).


These and other insights led to the conception of features such as ingredient limits and alerts, menu filtering by ingredient, and inclusion of uncommon allergens and other widely intolerable foods.


I also filmed a Cater prototype video that could be used for further evaluation with larger audiences.
