Here are some examples of my project work. While adhering to the process is ideal, I've found that projects aren't always cut and dry. This is why I evaluate every problem individually and remain open to multiple solutions.


Some efforts call for the entire UX design process, and others can be supplemented. Being effective requires an understanding of project needs as well as user requirements.

Cater is a contextually-aware recommendation app design that helps users balance their dietary restrictions with their unique dining preferences, offering users better recommendation listings and the ability to find meals they can enjoy in a variety of situations. The design of Cater was also my first opportunity to lead the entire UX process up to development.

Along with a team of students, from the Usability Research Methods course at the University of Washington, I conducted an extensive usability evaluation on the iOS and OSX Kindle Applications to uncover how users interact with and utilize the features of the application in support of a variety of reference and indexing goals.

The Smart City Greenhouse is a physical computing project that responds to changes in the environment and the needs of living plants. It is designed to harness the energy of the sun to drive the photosynthetic processes at work inside while responding to alert messages from the Smart City gateway library, designed and implemented by Andrew Davidson.

The Business Analytics Visualization was specifically designed for a local import and distribution company. It allows users to compare the sales performance of different salespeople and sales groups, to monitor sales account activity over time, and to  communicate important sales information to other stakeholders and salespeople.
